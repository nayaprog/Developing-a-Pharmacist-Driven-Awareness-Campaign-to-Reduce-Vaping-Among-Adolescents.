Developing a Pharmacist-Driven Awareness Campaign to Reduce Vaping Among Adolescents

Fast Facts You Should Know

  • One vape pod can deliver as much nicotine as 20 traditional cigarettes — exposing users to high risk of addiction.
  • Nicotine disrupts brain development in adolescents, affecting memory, attention, and impulse control.
  • Vaping releases harmful chemicals including formaldehyde, acrolein, and heavy metals that damage lung tissue and increase the risk of respiratory diseases.
  • Flavored vape products mask the harsh taste of nicotine and toxic substances, making them appealing to youth and increasing experimentation.
  • Pharmacists are accessible healthcare providers who offer counseling, nicotine replacement therapies, and support to help users quit vaping safely.

Health Risks of Vaping

Although marketed as a safer alternative to traditional cigarettes, vaping poses serious health risks:

  • Respiratory problems: Vaping aerosols contain harmful chemicals that cause lung irritation, inflammation, and increase susceptibility to infections.
  • Cardiovascular effects: Nicotine raises heart rate and blood pressure, increasing risk of heart disease and stroke over time.
  • Nicotine addiction: High nicotine concentrations in vape products create a strong dependency, making quitting difficult and increasing use of other tobacco products.
  • Brain development impact: Adolescents exposed to nicotine experience impaired attention, learning difficulties, and mood disorders.
  • Unknown long-term consequences: Vaping is a relatively new phenomenon, and ongoing studies suggest potential risks including lung diseases like EVALI (e-cigarette or vaping product use-associated lung injury).

Myths vs. Facts

  • Myth: Vaping produces only harmless water vapor.
    Fact: Aerosols contain toxic chemicals such as formaldehyde, acetaldehyde, and heavy metals like lead and nickel, which can cause lung and cardiovascular damage.
  • Myth: Vaping is safer than smoking cigarettes.
    Fact: While some harmful compounds are lower in vaping compared to cigarettes, vaping still poses significant health risks including nicotine addiction, lung inflammation, and possible long-term effects that are still being studied.
  • Myth: E-cigarettes are not addictive.
    Fact: Most vape products contain nicotine, one of the most addictive substances known, leading to dependence and increasing the likelihood of transitioning to traditional cigarette smoking.

Why This Advocacy Matters

The World Health Organization (WHO) identifies vaping as a growing public health concern, especially among adolescents, due to the high nicotine content and the presence of harmful substances in e-cigarette aerosols. Nicotine exposure during youth can cause long-lasting cognitive impairment and increase vulnerability to addiction to other substances.

The Department of Health (DOH) in the Philippines reports a rapid increase in e-cigarette use among Filipino teenagers, exacerbated by attractive flavors and aggressive marketing. Despite being promoted as a "safer alternative," vaping carries serious risks including respiratory issues, cardiovascular problems, and potential gateway effects to traditional tobacco smoking.

Interventions recommended by health authorities include:

  • Nicotine Replacement Therapy (NRT): Patches, gums, and lozenges provide controlled doses of nicotine to reduce withdrawal symptoms and cravings during cessation.
  • Behavioral counseling: Support from trained healthcare professionals such as pharmacists improves quit rates by addressing psychological and social triggers.
  • Quitlines and helplines: Free, confidential phone-based support services such as the Philippine Quitline help guide users through personalized quitting plans.
  • Public health campaigns: Educating communities about the dangers of vaping and enforcing age restrictions on sales help prevent initiation among youth.

Community pharmacists are frontline health workers uniquely positioned to educate, counsel, and provide cessation support, particularly in underserved areas. Their active involvement is crucial in the multi-sectoral effort to reduce vaping prevalence and improve public health outcomes.

How Nicotine Replacement Therapy (NRT) Works

Nicotine Replacement Therapy is a medically approved method to help users quit smoking or vaping by delivering controlled amounts of nicotine without harmful tobacco smoke. This helps to:

  • Reduce withdrawal symptoms such as irritability, anxiety, and cravings
  • Gradually decrease nicotine dependence in a safe and structured way
  • Improve success rates of quitting when combined with behavioral support

NRT products include nicotine patches, gums, lozenges, inhalers, and nasal sprays. These can be accessed at pharmacies, often with pharmacist counseling to choose the appropriate form and dosage.

Research shows that NRT doubles the chances of successfully quitting compared to no support. It is effective for both cigarette smokers and e-cigarette users aiming to break nicotine addiction.

Role of Pharmacists in Vaping Cessation

Pharmacists are among the most accessible healthcare professionals. Their role includes:

  • Providing evidence-based counseling on the risks of vaping and benefits of quitting
  • Assessing nicotine dependence and recommending suitable NRT options
  • Monitoring progress and managing side effects during cessation
  • Referring patients to additional medical or psychological support when needed
  • Participating in community education programs to raise awareness

Empowering pharmacists with training and resources enhances community efforts to reduce vaping-related harm and supports healthier futures.

Government Regulations and Policies

The Philippine government has taken steps to regulate the use and sale of e-cigarettes and vaping products, including:

  • Age restrictions prohibiting the sale of vaping products to individuals under 18 years old
  • Banning of flavored e-liquids that appeal to minors in some localities
  • Imposition of taxes and licensing requirements on vaping product manufacturers and retailers
  • Public smoking bans extended to include vaping in enclosed public spaces
  • Health warning labels required on product packaging

Continued enforcement and public education are essential to maximize these policies' effectiveness in protecting youth and vulnerable populations.

Resources & Support for Quitting

If you or someone you know wants to quit vaping, help is available:

  • Philippine Quitline: Call 1558 for free counseling and support.
  • Local Community Pharmacists: Ask for advice on NRT products and cessation plans.
  • Health Clinics and Hospitals: Seek medical advice for personalized treatment and counseling.
  • Online Support Groups: Join forums and social media groups dedicated to vaping cessation for peer encouragement.
  • Educational Websites: Visit WHO and DOH Philippines for up-to-date info and guidance.

Remember, quitting nicotine is challenging but possible. Utilize these resources and lean on healthcare professionals for support.
